Help and User Guide

Welcome to the Assemble Lesson Planner Help Page. Here you'll find detailed explanations of all fields and guidance on how to use the site effectively.

Lesson Plan Fields

  • Title: The name of your lesson plan. Make it descriptive and concise.
  • Program: Select the program under which this lesson falls, such as After School Program or Summer Camp.
  • Class Time: Choose the duration category that best fits your lesson.
  • Audience Grade: Specify the grade level(s) of the students.
  • Class Size: Indicate the expected number of students.
  • Subject Areas: Select all relevant subject areas, such as Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, or Mathematics.
  • Date & Time: Schedule the date and start time for the lesson.
  • Duration: Set the expected length of the lesson.
  • Location: Choose where the lesson will take place.
  • Learning Objectives: Outline what students should learn or achieve by the end of the lesson.
  • Objectives: Detailed goals for the lesson.
  • State/Common Core Standards: Reference any educational standards that the lesson meets.
  • Themes: Select any overarching themes related to the lesson content.
  • Lesson Overview: Provide a brief summary of the lesson.
  • Preparation Time: Estimate how much time is needed to prepare.
  • Instructional Methods: Choose the teaching strategies you'll use.
  • Differentiation Strategies: Describe how you'll adjust the lesson for diverse learners.
  • Assessment Methods: Select how you'll evaluate student learning.
  • Assessment: Details about assessments or evaluations.
  • Prerequisites: Any prior knowledge or skills students should have.
  • Safety Considerations: Notes on any safety precautions.
  • Technology Requirements: List any tech equipment or software needed.
  • Follow-up Activities: Suggestions for extensions or homework.
  • Feedback and Reflection: Space for notes after the lesson.
  • Vocabulary: Key terms and definitions.
  • Homework/Takeaways: Assignments or materials for students to take home.
  • Tasks: Select the tasks that will be part of the lesson.
  • Team Members: Add staff or volunteers involved in the lesson.
  • Partners: Include any external organizations collaborating on the lesson.
  • Materials Needed: List all materials required, along with preparation instructions.

Inventory Item Fields

  • Item Name: The name of the inventory item.
  • Description: A detailed description of the item.
  • Category: The category the item belongs to (e.g., Art Supplies, Electronics).
  • Item Image: Upload a photo of the item.
  • Inventory ID: A unique identifier for tracking.
  • Barcode: Barcode number if applicable.
  • Quantity in Stock: Current stock level.
  • Unit of Measurement: Units used to measure the item (e.g., pieces, sets).
  • Minimum Stock Level: The minimum quantity before reordering.
  • Maximum Stock Level: Maximum quantity to keep in stock.
  • Reorder Quantity: Quantity to order when restocking.
  • Supplier: The supplier's name.
  • Cost per Unit: Purchase price per unit.
  • Budget Category: The budget category for accounting purposes.
  • Storage Location: Where the item is stored.
  • Shelf Life/Expiration Date: Applicable for perishable items.
  • Storage Conditions: Any special storage requirements.
  • Last Used Date: When the item was last used.
  • Usage Frequency: How often the item is used.
  • Assigned To: If the item is allocated to a person or program.
  • Condition Status: Current condition of the item.
  • Maintenance Requirements: Notes on maintaining the item.
  • Repair History: Any past repairs.
  • Safety Data Sheets (SDS): Upload SDS if applicable.
  • Compliance Notes: Any compliance-related information.
  • Notes: Additional notes about the item.
  • Attachments: Upload any related documents.

Using the Site

To get started, you can navigate to the Lesson Plans or Inventory sections from the navigation bar.

Creating a Lesson Plan

  1. Click on "Lesson Plans" in the navigation bar.
  2. Click on "Create New Lesson Plan."
  3. Fill in all the required fields. Fields marked with an asterisk (*) are mandatory.
  4. Add materials needed by clicking "Add Material" in the Materials Preparation section.
  5. Once all information is entered, click "Save" to create the lesson plan.

Managing Inventory

  1. Click on "Inventory" in the navigation bar.
  2. View the list of inventory items and their stock levels.
  3. To add a new item, click on "Add Inventory Item."
  4. Fill in all the required fields and click "Save."
  5. To update stock levels, navigate to the item detail page and use the "Add Stock" or "Remove Stock" options.

Need Further Assistance?

If you have any questions or encounter issues, please contact the administrator or refer to the documentation provided.
